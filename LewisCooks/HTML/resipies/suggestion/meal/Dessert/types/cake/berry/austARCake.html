Prep Time: 25 minutes
Cook Time: 50 minutes- 1 hour

What you need... A scale and this...

     1 large cooking apple peeled cored and thickly sliced
     100 butter sofened
     100 grams of light muscovado sugar
     1 teaspoon(s) bicarbonate of soda
     175 grams self-raising flour
     1 teaspoon(s) cocoa powder
     0.5 teaspoon(s) ground cinnamon
     0.5 teaspoon(s) freshly ground nutmeg
     75 raisins
      a little bit of sugar for dusting

How To Do It

    1.   Lightly grease the tin and line the base with a circle of non-stick baking parchment. Pre-heat the oven to 325F. Put the apple slicesinto a small pan add a very little water and cook gently until tender. Stir the apple mixture to break it down a little, but it doesn't neet to be a smooth puree. Remove from the heat and leave until just warm.
    2.   Put the butter and sugar into a large bowl and beat together util evenly blended; an electric mixer is best for this but of course you can also beat by hand with a wooden spoon. Mix together the warm apple and the bicarbonate of soda in a small bowl(the mixture with froth up, but don't worry: this is quite normal). Add the butter and sugar and stir to mix.
    3.   Sift the flour, cocoa powder, nutmeg and cinnamon into the mixture, add the raisins and fold in gently, using a plastic spatula or a large metal spoon. Turninto the prepaired cake tin and gently level the surface with a spatula or the back of a spoon.
    3.   Bake in the middle of the oven for 50 minutes-1 hour until the cake is golden brown in color, spingy to the touch and has begun to shrinkk away from the side of the tin. Allow to cool inthe tin for 5-10 minutes, then turn it out, remove the pase paper and leave on wire rack to cool completely. Dust lightly with icing before searving.
