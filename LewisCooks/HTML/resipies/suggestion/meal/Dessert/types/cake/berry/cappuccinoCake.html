Prep Time: 20 minutes
Cook Time: 25-30 minutes

What you need... A scale and this...

     50 grams cocoa powder
     6 tablespoons boiling water
     3 eggs
     60 ml milk
     175 grams self rising flour
     1 rounded teaspoon(s) baking powder
     100 grams margarin or butter, soffened
      275 grams caster sugar
    for the filling and topping:
     300 ml double cream
     1 teaspoon(s) instant coffee, dissovled in 2 teaspoons hot water
     a little cocoa power or drinking chocolate for dusting

How To Do It

    1.   Lightly grease the tins and line the bases with non-stick baking parchment. Pre-heat the oven to 350F. Put the cocoa powder into a large mixing bowl add the boiling water and mix well until it has a paste-like consistency.
    2.   Add all the remaining ingredient to the bowl and whistl with a hand-held electric mixer until just combined. The mixture will be a thickish batter(be careful not to over whisk).
    3.   Divide the cake mixture between the prepared tins and gently level the surface. Bake for 25-30 minutes, until the cakes are well risen and beginning to shrink away from the side of the tin. Turn the cakes out on a wire rack and leave to cool completely.
    4.   To finish the cake, whip the double cream until it just holds its shape and then stir n the dissolved coffee. Use half of the cream to fill the cake and spread the remainder over the top. Gently smooth the surface with a palette knife and dust with sifted cocoa or drinking chocolate. This cake is best eaten fresh; store in the fridge if necessary.
